Chandrayaan-2

Chandrayaan-2, launched in July 2019, was the follow-up mission to Chandrayaan-1 and a major leap forward in India's lunar exploration efforts. It aimed to conduct detailed studies of the Moon, including its south polar region. The mission consisted of an orbiter, a lander named Vikram, and a rover named Pragyan.

The orbiter, equipped with a suite of scientific instruments, continues to orbit the Moon and transmit crucial data back to Earth. However, during the final stages of the mission, the lander experienced a hard landing on the lunar surface, and the rover's mission was cut short.

Despite the partial success of the lander and rover components, the Chandrayaan-2 orbiter remains operational and continues to contribute valuable information about the Moon's surface and atmosphere.

Chandrayaan-2 represented India's determination and advancements in space technology, further solidifying its position in the global space arena and laying the groundwork for future ambitious space missions.
