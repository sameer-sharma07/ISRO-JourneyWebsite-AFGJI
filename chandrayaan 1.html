Chandrayaan-1

Chandrayaan-1 was India's first lunar mission, launched by ISRO in October 2008. It marked a significant milestone in India's space exploration journey. The mission aimed to study the Moon's surface and gather valuable scientific data. Chandrayaan-1 carried eleven scientific instruments, including a high-resolution camera, a radar mapper, and a Moon Mineralogy Mapper (M3) provided by NASA.During its mission, Chandrayaan-1 discovered water molecules on the Moon's surface, specifically in the polar regions. The presence of water was a groundbreaking revelation with potential implications for future lunar missions and space exploration. Unfortunately, the mission ended prematurely in August 2009 when communication with the spacecraft was lost. Despite its shorter than expected operational life, Chandrayaan-1 paved the way for subsequent lunar missions and strengthened India's position in the global space community.
